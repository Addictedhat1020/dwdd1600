About Categories

Low powered blasters
In the low powered blasters category you will find low powered blasters that don't hurt too bad when you get shot. These blasters are used for low FPS nerf wars, target shooting, and more.
High powered blasters
In the high powered blasters category you will find high powered blaster that will hurt when you get shot. These blasters can be used for competitions, high FPS nerf wars, and more.
Target shooting blasters
In the target shooting blasters category you will find blasters that good at shooting targets. These blasters are usually not too good in nerf wars or much else so they are used for mainly target shooting.
Miscellaneous
In the miscellaneous category you will find anything that does not go in any of the categories or useful information.

History of Nerf

The popular dart blaster brand Nerf was started by Reyn Guyer and they made foam balls. He made the hit Nerf Ball and relesed it in 1969. He then went on to make a blaster that shot the Nerf Ball. He eventually made blasters that shot foam darts.

History of Nerf Blasters

In 2008 the N-Strike blaster line was made and shaped the modern blasters we know today.The N-Strike Elite blaster line launched in 2012 improved the N-Strike blasters.

History of High powered blasters.

Eventually 3D printed high powered blasters started to catch on and there was lots of high powered 3D printed blasters being made. In 2020 the revolutionary Nexus Pro was made by Dart Zone and it changed the course of dart blaster history. The Nexus Pro was the first high powered blaster to be sold on shelves with no assembly required. You could buy it off the shelf and take it straight to a nerf war and be able to use it without putting it together first. The Nexus Pro had the option to shoot short darts that are able to fly faster and farther. The Aeon Pro was released shortly after the Nexus Pro and was a smaller version of the Nexus Pro with the same power. Other blaster companies started making high powered blasters and eventually in 2023 Nerf released the Strife X with there own short darts. Dart zone made a lot more high powered blasters after the Nexus Pro like the MK 1.1, MK 2, MK 3, and tons more blasters you can check out on their website.

Types of darts

In the dart blasting hobby there are lots of different types of darts you can use. Not all darts are compatable with different blasters. Here is a list of the main types of darts:

  • Long darts
  • Short darts
  • Foam balls
  • Mega darts

There are lots of different types and brands of darts that can go with these main categories.

Long darts

  1. Nerf elite darts
  2. Dart zone waffle tip darts
  3. Nerf accustrike darts
  4. Dart zone chili darts
  5. Dart zone ember standard-length darts
  6. Dart zone ruby standard-length darts
  7. Dart zone bamboo standard-length darts

Short darts

Foam balls

  2. Nerf Mega accustrike darts
