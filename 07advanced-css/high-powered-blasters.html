High Powered Blasters

History Of High Powered Blasters

High powered blasters first started in the Nerf hobby when people were making 3D printed blasters that shot cut-down long darts. Dart Zone came in in 2019 when they relesed the MK1. It was the first blaster you could buy from a blaster company that was injection molded plastic. In 2020 the Nexus Pro was released on shelves and was the first high powered blaster you could buy on shelf.

High Powered Blasters

There are lots of good high powered blasters you can get so here are some of the best in my opinion.

The Nexus Pro
The Nexus Pro is a very good, cheap blaster to get you into high powered blasting.
The MK2
The MK2 is a small, compact pistol blaster that is a very good secondary.
The Tomcat
The Tomcat is a good springer blaster that has a 50 round short dart drum.
The MK3
The MK3 is a flywheel blaster that is good a single shot flywheel blaster.
The MK2.1
The MK2.1 is a magazine fed pistol blaster that is a good secondary and primary.
The Solo Pro
The Solo Pro is a very good compact backup blaster.

Purchase Recommendations

I would recommend getting whatever you need. There are tons of different pro blasters for almost every play style and need. If you need a primary springer then you can get the Nexus Pro and if you need a primary flywheel then you can get the MK3 and so on.

The Nexus Pro

The Nexus Pro is a very good, cheap primary springer and is a very good way to get into high powered blasting.

The Tomcat

The Tomcat is a good high capacity springer.

The MK 2

The MK 2 is a very good compact secondary blaster

The Solo Pro

The Solo Pro is a very good compact backup blaster that still hits hard.
